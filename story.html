Our Story

Ninety years of family craft. One cup at a time.

The Beginning

My family has been farming coffee in Boquete, Panama for over 90 years.

Long before the region became world-famous — before international auctions, before Geisha coffee captivated the specialty world — the Gonzalez family was already cultivating beans in the same volcanic highlands, guided by the same morning mists and the same generational instinct for quality.

I'm Alex Evans, and I grew up hearing those stories. The careful hand-sorting of cherries. The rhythms of harvest season. The pride in a bean grown slowly, at altitude, in soil that has been worked and understood across generations. Coffee wasn't just a crop in my family — it was a craft, a culture, and a calling.

"Coffee wasn't just a crop in my family — it was a craft, a culture, and a calling."

The Land

Every bean we source carries a family name.

Café Boquete was born from that inheritance. Every coffee we source comes from land my family owns today, or land my grandfather once farmed with his own hands. These are not anonymous origins or distant suppliers — they are fields with family names attached, worked by people we know and trust, on soil our family has tended for nearly a century.

What arrives in your cup has a name, a place, and a lineage that goes back generations.

1930s

Finca Gonzalez is established

The Gonzalez family begins farming coffee in the volcanic highlands of Boquete, Chiriquí — decades before the region gains international recognition.

Generations

The craft is passed down

Harvest rhythms, processing traditions, and deep knowledge of the land pass from one generation to the next — long before specialty coffee terminology existed.

Today

Café Boquete brings it to Portland

Alex Evans founds Café Boquete Coffee Company — sourcing exclusively from family-owned and family-connected land in Boquete, bridging two continents with one cup.
